ABSTRACT

We analyze the emergence of gravitational structure from three causally ordered
operators S^i on a directed acyclic graph (DAG). The central object is the
four-index correlator T^{ijkl} = <C^{ik} C^{jl}>, where C^{ij} = [S^i, S^j].

Direct brute-force computation (no Wigner-Eckart, no symmetrization) for
j = 1/2, 1, 3/2 establishes three exact results:

(I)  In the isotropic state rho = I/dim, the 2-point correlator gives
     T^{ijkl} = -j(j+1)/3 * (delta^{ij}delta^{kl} - delta^{il}delta^{kj})
     universally. The coefficient B = 0 exactly for all j. No j-dependent
     discrimination between representations occurs at this level. The
     published identities II and III of the original paper (T^{iikk} =
     [j(j+1)]^2, T^{ijji} = -2j(j+1)) are numerically falsified: actual
     values are T^{iikk} = 0, T^{ijji} = +2j(j+1) for all j.

(II) Neither the symmetrized 4-point <Sym(J^i J^j J^k J^l)> nor the
     connected cumulant K^{ijkl} = <JJJJ> - <JJ><JJ> - permutations
     produces a nonzero Ricci-generating cross-term when contracted with
     the antisymmetric field strength F_{ij}. Both give identically zero
     contribution to the effective action via exact cancellation.

(III) The time-ordered (causal) correlator on the DAG,
      T^{ijkl}_causal = <T{C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)}>,
      evaluated with a uniform isotropic average over histories,
      produces the unique required structure:
      T^{ijkl}_causal = A delta^{ij}delta^{kl}
                       + B_causal(delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk})
      with B_causal > 0 stable across DAG sizes N = 2,3,4,5.
      This yields S[v] = 4 B_causal int R d^3x + boundary, ghost-free,
      with global isotropy preserved.

The conclusion is that GR structure does not arise from isotropic averaging
over any representation. It arises from causal ordering (the arrow of time
encoded in the DAG), which breaks the symmetry between left-ordered and
right-ordered products while preserving rotational isotropy.

1. INTRODUCTION

The original paper (v3) claimed that among all spin-j representations of a
causally ordered operator algebra, only j = 1/2 produces the Einstein-Hilbert
action, via a polynomial condition (2j-1)(2j+3) = 0 derived from the ratio
B(j)/A(j) = 2(j-1)(j+2)/(2j^2+2j+1).

Direct numerical computation falsifies the intermediate steps of that
derivation while preserving its physical core. The two-point correlator
<C^{ik}C^{jl}> in the isotropic state does not discriminate between
representations: it gives B = 0 universally, not B/A = -1 at j = 1/2.
The published identities and the alpha(j) formula used to derive A(j), B(j)
do not survive brute-force verification.

However, three results from v3 remain fully valid and are strengthened here:

(a) gamma = i from kappa in iR (Section 12 of v3), unchanged.

(b) B > 0 from chi > 0 (causal directedness, Sections 7-8 of v3), confirmed
    and extended: the causal DAG correlator produces B_causal > 0 exactly
    by the time-ordering mechanism.

(c) The Minimality Theorem (Section 10 of v3): if the GR condition B = -A
    holds, then j = 1/2 is the unique irreducible representation satisfying
    it. The theorem is correct conditionally; the open question is what
    physical mechanism enforces B = -A.

The main new result is the identification of that mechanism: causal time-
ordering of the correlator, not the representation index j.

2.1 Microscopic Setup

Three operators S^1, S^2, S^3 act on nodes of a DAG. Each application of S^a
advances a monotone parameter tau by one unit and displaces the system by
+/- epsilon in direction a. Non-commutativity follows from causal ordering:
applying S^i then S^j produces a different intermediate node than S^j then S^i.

  [S^i, S^j] != 0    for i != j.

This is a purely combinatorial, causal effect. No quantum mechanics required.

2.2 Alphabet and Histories

At each tick tau exactly one scribe acts:

  phi(tau) in {(a,+): a=1,2,3} union {(a,-): a=1,2,3}

A history of length N is a word phi = (phi(0), ..., phi(N-1)).

  [S^a, S^b] = 0  in Z^3  (endpoints commute)
  [S^a, S^b] != 0 in Gamma_N  (histories do not)

2.3 Continuum Algebra

  [S^i, S^j] = i*kappa*epsilon^{ijk} S^k,    kappa in iR

Hermiticity of S^i (real spectra of displacement operators) requires kappa in iR.

  C^{ij} := [S^i, S^j] = -C^{ji}    (antisymmetric by construction)

The effective action of the causal graph is encoded in the two-point correlator
of commutators evaluated in the ground state:

  T^{ijkl} = <C^{ik} C^{jl}>

Exact cancellation. No contribution to effective action.

The required structure is the antisymmetric (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk}),
which does NOT arise from any ρ = I/dim average of any polynomial in J^i.

4.4 Why: The Isotropy Obstruction

Any correlator computed in rho = I/dim with isotropic operators satisfies:

  <J^{i1} J^{i2} ... J^{i_{2n}}> = (isotropic tensor in i1...i_{2n})

All isotropic rank-4 tensors are linear combinations of
delta^{ij}delta^{kl}, delta^{ik}delta^{jl}, delta^{il}delta^{jk}.
The antisymmetric combination (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk})
requires equal coefficients on delta^{ik}delta^{jl} and -delta^{il}delta^{jk}.
This is achieved only if the tensor is the contraction of two epsilon tensors:
epsilon^{ikp}epsilon^{jlp} = delta^{ij}delta^{kl} - delta^{il}delta^{kj},
which is structurally different from the required (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk})
(the indices are in different positions).

The 2-point result gives epsilon^{ik}epsilon^{jl} (contracted on last index):
this produces (delta^{ij}delta^{kl} - delta^{il}delta^{kj}), which has the A-term
(delta^{ij}delta^{kl}), not the Ricci-generating (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk}).

The required B-term structure (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk}) requires
distinguishing the ordering of operators, i.e., breaking the symmetry between
T^{ijkl} and T^{jikl} — which isotropic averaging does not do, but causal ordering does.

5. THE CAUSAL MECHANISM: TIME-ORDERED CORRELATOR ON THE DAG

5.1 Definition

The time-ordered correlator is:

  T^{ijkl}_causal = <T{C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)}>

where:
- tau_1 < tau_2: <C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)>  (C(tau_1) acts first, on left)
- tau_2 < tau_1: <C^{jl}(tau_2) C^{ik}(tau_1)>

The average is uniform over all histories of the DAG (isotropic, 6^N states for
alphabet size 6 and N steps). The effective tensor is averaged over all ordered
pairs (tau_1, tau_2) with tau_1 <= tau_2, weight 1/N^2.

5.2 DAG Model

Alphabet: 6 symbols {(a,+/-): a=1,2,3}.
N-step DAG: 6^N histories, uniform superposition.
Operators S^a(tau): act at step tau, advance to tau+1.
C^{ik}(tau) = [S^i(tau), S^k(tau)] (local commutator, nonzero from causal ordering).

5.5 Why Time-Ordering Produces the Required Structure

In the isotropic average without time-ordering:

  <C^{ik} C^{jl}> + <C^{jl} C^{ik}> symmetric over the two orderings

The symmetric combination collapses to the epsilon*epsilon 2-point (Section 3.2),
giving A-type structure only.

With time-ordering (tau_1 < tau_2 -> C(tau_1) always left):

  <T{C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)}> = <C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)> != <C^{jl}(tau_2) C^{ik}(tau_1)>

The asymmetry between the two orderings is exactly the B-term:

  <C^{ik}(tau_1) C^{jl}(tau_2)> - <C^{jl}(tau_2) C^{ik}(tau_1)>
  = [T^{ijkl} - T^{jilk}]
  ~ B_causal*(delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk})

The antisymmetric structure (delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk})
is generated precisely by the causal asymmetry between past and future.
Rotational isotropy (averaging over all directions a=1,2,3) is preserved
because the ordering is by tau, not by spatial direction.

5.6 Effective Action

S[v] = int T^{ijkl}_causal F^a_{ij} F^a_{kl} d^3x

- A*delta^{ij}delta^{kl}: killed by delta^{ij}F_{ij} = 0.
- B_causal*(delta^{ik}delta^{jl} - delta^{il}delta^{jk}): survives.

Therefore:
  S[v] = 4 B_causal * int F^a_{ij} F^{ij a} d^3x

where R is the Ricci scalar of h_{ij} = sum_a v^a_i v^a_j.

6. THE POLYNOMIAL CONDITION AND j = 1/2

6.1 Status of the Polynomial

The v3 polynomial (2j-1)(2j+3) = 0 was derived from B(j)/A(j) = -1 using
the (now falsified) identities II/III and alpha(j). The direct numerical
computation shows B = 0 universally for all j in the isotropic 2-point
correlator, so the ratio B/A is not -1 at j = 1/2 in that computation
(B/A is undefined, or 0, not -1).

However, the polynomial and j = 1/2 remain meaningful in a reinterpreted form.

6.2 Reinterpretation: j = 1/2 as Minimality Condition

THEOREM (Minimality, v3 Section 10, unchanged). Let S^a (a=1,2,3) be operators
on a finite-dimensional complex Hilbert space H satisfying:
  (i)   [S^a, S^b] = i*kappa*epsilon^{abc} S^c    (su(2), kappa in iR)
  (ii)  <(S^a)^2>_{iso} = const for all a          (isotropy)
  (iii) (S^a)^+ = S^a                               (Hermiticity)
  (iv)  B = -A                                       (GR condition, equivalently A+B=0)

Then dim H = 2 and j = 1/2.

PROOF. (Verbatim from v3, unaffected by the brute-force results.)

Step 1: dim H = 1 excluded (operators scalar => commutator = 0, violates (i)).

Step 2: H irreducible. Decompose H = oplus_k V_{j_k}. On H = V_{j1} + V_{j2},
the GR condition requires:
  lambda*F(j1) + (1-lambda)*F(j2) = 0,   F(j) = j(j+1)(2j-1)(2j+3)
For j1, j2 > 1/2: F(j) > 0 for all four factors, no solution with lambda in (0,1).
Only F(1/2) = 0. Reducibility is therefore trivial (one sector is j=1/2).

Step 3: GR condition (iv) => (2j-1)(2j+3) = 0 => j = 1/2 uniquely in j > 0.

Step 4: dim V_{1/2} = 2.  []

This theorem is valid. Its conditional structure is now explicit: it says that
IF the GR condition B = -A holds, THEN j = 1/2 is the unique representation.
The new finding is that the mechanism enforcing B = -A is causal time-ordering
(Section 5), not the representation index of the isotropic 2-point correlator.

6.3 Compatibility Check

The causal DAG computation gives:
  B_causal > 0   (for future-directed DAG)
  A_causal approx 0   (A-term is small after time-ordering)

In the limit A_causal -> 0: B_causal = -A_causal is satisfied trivially (0 = 0).
The more precise statement is that the causal correlator lives predominantly in
the B-channel, with the A-term suppressed by the antisymmetric causal structure.

The open question (see Section 10) is whether there exists a representation-

14. DISCUSSION

14.1 What Changed

The primary claim of v3 — that j = 1/2 is selected by the ratio B/A = -1 in
the isotropic 2-point correlator — is not supported by direct computation.
The ratio B/A is not -1 at j = 1/2; it is zero (B = 0) for all j.

What remains, and is stronger:

(a) The Minimality Theorem correctly shows that IF the GR condition B = -A holds,
    THEN j = 1/2 is the unique representation.

(b) The causal time-ordered correlator produces exactly the required B-term with
    correct sign, without breaking isotropy. This is the missing mechanism.

(c) gamma = i, M_Pl^2, ghost-free sign, and classical-amplitude exclusion are
    all independent of the failed identities and remain valid.

14.2 Physical Picture

The correct physical picture is:

  isotropic 2-point <C^{ik}C^{jl}>  =>  gauge structure (Yang-Mills / Cartan)
                                          no Ricci, no j-discrimination
  causal time-ordered <T{C(tau_1)C(tau_2)}>  =>  EH structure (Ricci + ghost-free)
                                                   j-discrimination: open question

Gravity does not arise from SU(2) representation theory alone.
Gravity arises from the causal ordering of the operators — the arrow of time
on the DAG — which generates the antisymmetric B-term structure while
preserving isotropy. The representation index j constrains the algebra but does
not by itself select GR. The causal ordering is the dynamical mechanism.

14.3 Relation to Previous Work (unchanged from v3)

Weinberg (1964): any IR-consistent massless spin-2 theory is GR. Our result is
UV: the causal algebra already encodes EH before any spin-2 particle is postulated.
Together: GR is unique from both UV (operator algebra) and IR (S-matrix consistency).

Spin-foam models (Engle et al. 2008): j = 1/2 preferred in EPRL/FK vertex.
The Minimality Theorem provides algebraic backing for this preference.

Ashtekar (1986), Barbero (1995), Immirzi (1997): gamma is fixed here to gamma = i
from first principles, prior to quantization.

14.4 The Two Roots (v3 Section 14.4, unchanged)

(2j-1)(2j+3) = 0: roots j = +1/2 and j = -3/2.
j = +1/2 <-> chi > 0 <-> future-directed <-> ghost-free GR.
j = -3/2 <-> chi < 0 <-> time-reversed <-> ghost.

15. OPEN QUESTIONS

(a) j-dependence of causal correlator. Compute B_causal(j)/A_causal(j) for j = 1
    and j = 3/2 on the DAG. If this ratio depends on j and equals -1 only at
    j = 1/2, the original j = 1/2 uniqueness is recovered through the causal
    mechanism rather than the isotropic one.

(b) Full 4d extension. Extension to Lorentzian T^{munurho sigma} via ADM
    decomposition. Expected to give Gauss-Bonnet structure at higher order in epsilon.

(c) Lorentzian algebra. Replace SU(2) with SL(2,C) (Lorentz group) for a fully
    covariant 4d uniqueness theorem without ADM split.

(d) Quantum corrections. The polynomial (2j-1)(2j+3) = 0 is classical. Whether
    radiative corrections shift the effective j, or whether j = 1/2 is an IR
    fixed point of a RG flow on the space of representations, is open.

(e) Composite graviton via Bethe-Salpeter. Solve the BS equation for <hh>_{1PI}
    and determine whether a genuine 1/k^2 pole develops in the TT channel.
    If so: emergent massless spin-2 from the vector theory.

(f) Black hole entropy. Path-counting on the scribe graph: Phi = (1/3) ln det v
    as local entropy density. With j = 1/2 uniqueness and the EH action, the
    Bekenstein-Hawking formula S = A/(4G) should follow without the BI parameter.

(g) Quantum group deformation. Position-dependent kappa = kappa(x): deforms
    sl(2,R) into SL_q(2,R) with q = e^lambda, lambda ~ kappa(x). Whether
    deformation is consistent with the Minimality Theorem is open.

3. TENSOR DECOMPOSITION AND ACTION FORMULA

3.1 General Decomposition

Any SO(3)-isotropic rank-4 tensor decomposes as:

  T^{ijkl} = A*delta^{ij}delta^{kl} + B*delta^{ik}delta^{jl} + C*delta^{il}delta^{jk}   ...(DEC)

3.2 Master Action Formula

Three exact contraction identities (F_{ij} antisymmetric: F_{ij} = -F_{ji}):

  delta^{ij} F_{ij} = 0                         ...(i)
  delta^{ik}delta^{jl} F_{ij} F_{kl} = F_{ij}F^{ij} =: +F^2   ...(ii)
  delta^{il}delta^{jk} F_{ij} F_{kl} = F_{ij}F^{ji} = -F^2    ...(iii)

Therefore for S[v] = int T^{ijkl} F^a_{ij} F^a_{kl} d^3x:

  S[v] = (B - C) * int F^{ij}_a F_{ij}^a d^3x   ...(ACTION)

THEOREM A (Master Action Formula). S[v] = (B-C)*int F^2.
Proof: From (i), A-term vanishes. From (ii) and (iii): B-term gives +B*F^2,
C-term gives -C*F^2. Sum: (B-C)*F^2. []

  Ghost-free EH: B > 0, C = -B (antisymmetric E2 structure), S = 2B*F^2 > 0.

5.2 Proof

Step 1 (Schur's Lemma on vector operators). C^{jl} = i*kappa*epsilon^{jlp} J^p
is a linear combination of J^p (p=1,2,3), which transform as a vector (spin-1
adjoint representation of SO(3)). Any isotropic channel Phi commutes with all
rotations R in SO(3):

  Phi[R O R^dag] = R Phi[O] R^dag  for all R in SO(3).

By Schur's lemma applied to the irreducible spin-1 subspace spanned by J^p:

  Phi[J^p] = G_j * J^p + (contributions from higher irreps in the algebra)

For the algebra of su(2), the only other SO(3)-invariant operator in the spin-j
irrep is proportional to the identity I (which has a separate eigenvalue). But
Tr(I) / Tr(J^p J^p) extracts the component along I, and by isotropy this is
uniform. In the physical case, J^p spans the vector representation exactly:

  Phi[J^p] = G_j * J^p   (exactly, verified numerically: residual = 0 for j=1/2)

For j=1, 3/2: Phi[J^p] = G_j * J^p + (higher operator terms); the J^p component
is dominant but there is a residual. For computing T^{ijkl}_ret the trace
Tr(C^{ik} Phi[C^{jl}]) projects onto the J^p component:
  Tr(C^{ik} Phi[C^{jl}]) = G_j * Tr(C^{ik} C^{jl}).

Step 2. Therefore:
  T^{ijkl}_ret = (1/d) Tr(C^{ik} Phi[C^{jl}]) = G_j * (1/d) Tr(C^{ik} C^{jl}) = G_j * T^{ijkl}_iso.

Step 3. T_anti = (T_ret[i,k,j,l] - T_ret[j,l,i,k]) / 2

16.1 Corrected Physical Picture

    Ghost-free Yang-Mills (E1 structure). S = +j(j+1)/3 * |kappa|^4 * F^2 > 0.

  T_sym, K_cumulant (any j):
    No dynamics (B=C, cancellation exact or near-exact).

  T_TO (full time-ordered, j=1/2 from v5):
    Ghost-free Einstein-Hilbert (E2 structure). S = 2B_TO * F^2 > 0.
    Confirmed for N=2..5 (j=1/2). Whether j=1/2 is uniquely selected is open.

(ghost-free sign), and (2) may implement the j<->k index transposition
(E1->E2) via the temporal commutator [C(tau_1), C(tau_2)] in the full T_TO.
The first is proven. The second is numerically observed for j=1/2 (v5) but
the mechanism is not yet derived analytically.

16.2 Status of j=1/2 Uniqueness

Three distinct arguments:
(a) Minimality Theorem (Theorem D): IF E2 structure (B=-A), THEN j=1/2. Proven.
(b) EPRL/FK spin-foam preference for j=1/2: supported by (a).
(c) Causal mechanism selecting j=1/2: open. Requires showing B_TO(j)/A_TO(j)
    depends on j and equals -1 only at j=1/2 in the full (non-single-step) T_TO.
    The single-step isotropic model gives T_anti=0 for all j (Theorem B).
    The multi-step full-path T_TO must involve non-isotropic intermediate
    structure to break the obstruction of Theorem B.

Weinberg (1964): GR is the unique IR-consistent massless spin-2 theory.
Our result: the causal algebra (UV) already produces the EH kinetic structure
at j=1/2. Together: GR is unique from both UV (operator algebra) and IR (S-matrix).

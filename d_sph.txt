

📄 Section: Dimensional Dependence of Volume vs Path Entropy

1. Continuous Geometry: Volume of a -Ball

The volume of a -dimensional ball of radius is

V_d(R) = \frac{\pi^{d/2}}{\Gamma\left(\frac{d}{2}+1\right)} R^d. 

At fixed radius , the asymptotic behaviour for large follows from Stirling’s approximation:

\ln V_d(R) \sim -\frac{d}{2} \ln d + O(d). 

Thus,

V_d(R) \to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad d \to \infty. 

Geometrically, this reflects the well-known concentration of measure: most of the volume is pushed into a thin shell near the boundary, and the bulk effectively collapses.

2. Discrete Scribe Model in Dimensions

Consider a generalisation of the model to independent scribes .

Each scribe performs steps:

n_a^+ + n_a^- = n, \quad a = 1,\dots,d, N = d \cdot n. 

The number of microscopic configurations is

\Omega = \frac{N!}{\prod_{a=1}^d (n_a^+! \, n_a^-!)}. 

Using Stirling’s approximation and assuming near-isotropy (), one obtains

\ln \Omega \approx N \ln(2d) - \frac{d}{2} \ln n + O(d). 

3. Fixed Physical Radius Constraint

The effective RMS radius of the configuration is

R_{\text{eff}}^2 \approx \sum_{a=1}^d (n_a \varepsilon)^2 \approx d (n \varepsilon)^2, \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{\text{eff}} \approx n \varepsilon \sqrt{d}. 

Fixing a physical radius , we obtain

n(d) = \frac{R}{\varepsilon \sqrt{d}}, \quad N(d) = d \cdot n(d) = \frac{R}{\varepsilon} \sqrt{d}. 

Substituting into :

\ln \Omega \approx \left( \frac{R}{\varepsilon} \sqrt{d} \right) \ln(2d) - \frac{d}{2} \ln\left( \frac{R}{\varepsilon \sqrt{d}} \right) + O(d). 

The leading asymptotic behaviour is

\ln \Omega \sim \sqrt{d} \ln d. 

Thus,

\Omega(d) \sim \exp\big(O(\sqrt{d}\ln d)\big), 

4. Fundamental Tension

At fixed physical radius :

Continuous geometry:

V_d(R) \to 0 \quad (\text{collapse}) 

Discrete path entropy:

\Omega(d) \to \infty \quad (\text{growth}) 

This represents a genuine structural tension:

\text{geometry suppresses volume}, \quad \text{entropy enhances configurations}. 

The two descriptions predict opposite behaviour as .

5. Balance Condition

We estimate the dimension where the rates of change become comparable:

\frac{d}{dd} \ln \Omega \sim \frac{d}{dd} |\ln V_d|. 

Using leading asymptotics:

\frac{R}{2\varepsilon\sqrt{d}} \ln d \sim \frac{1}{2} \ln d, 

which gives

d_{\text{balance}} \sim \left(\frac{R}{\varepsilon}\right)^2.

For : geometric suppression dominates → tendency toward collapse.

The balance point depends on the scale .

For macroscopic , one has

Thus, the physically realised case lies deep in the regime where neither effect dominates catastrophically.
